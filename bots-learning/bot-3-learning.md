## Key Learnings
- Mediterranean, DASH, and plant-based diets are associated with reduced risk of cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes.
- Ultra-processed food consumption correlates with adverse metabolic outcomes regardless of macronutrient ratios.
- Dietary pattern adherence scores (e.g., HEI-2020, MED score) are more predictive of long-term health than single-nutrient analysis.

## Research Findings
- Cohort studies show Mediterranean diet adherence reduces all-cause mortality by ~9% (PREDIMED trial data).
- DASH diet lowers systolic blood pressure by an average of 8–14 mmHg in hypertensive individuals.
- Intermittent fasting patterns show metabolic benefits comparable to continuous caloric restriction in short-term trials.
