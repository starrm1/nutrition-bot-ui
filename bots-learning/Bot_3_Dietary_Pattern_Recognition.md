# Bot 3 - Dietary Pattern Recognition

## Understanding common dietary patterns and their health outcomes

### Description
A detailed exploration of prevalent dietary patterns, their characteristics, and the associated health outcomes. The goal is to enable better dietary choices based on scientific data.

### Progress
- **Progress Percentage:** 42%
- **Phase:** advanced_learning

### Key Learnings
- Mediterranean and DASH diets are consistently associated with reduced cardiovascular disease risk.
- Plant-based dietary patterns lower all-cause mortality when protein sources are diverse.
- Ultra-processed food consumption is the strongest dietary predictor of obesity and type-2 diabetes risk.
- Meal timing and circadian alignment significantly modify the metabolic impact of dietary patterns.

### Research Findings
- Mediterranean diet adherence reduces CVD events by ~30% (PREDIMED trial).
- DASH diet lowers systolic blood pressure by 8–14 mmHg in hypertensive individuals.
- Western dietary pattern (high sugar, refined carbs, red meat) correlates with systemic inflammation markers (CRP, IL-6).
- Identified 7 major global dietary patterns through cluster analysis of nutritional epidemiology datasets.

### Current Focus Areas
- Developing a pattern-scoring algorithm for real-time dietary assessment.
- Mapping regional and cultural dietary variants within recognized pattern clusters.
- Investigating how dietary patterns interact with genetic predispositions (overlap with Bot 1 Nutrigenomics).
